Telemetron

During the Spring 2021 semester I worked with the MIT Media Lab to create Telemetron: a musical instrument designed for space. The Telemetron uses an IMU and produces a sound corresponding to the orientation of the instrument. I worked on the mechanical design of the instrument, taking into consideration numerous constraints including size, electronic component placement, and aesthetic.

The Telemetron took part in a zero gravity flight on May 23, 2021, and will hopefully travel to the ISS sometime in 2022.

Step 1: Design in Fusion 360

Before designing the outer shell of the Telemetron, the inner structure needed to be finalized. The inner structure holds a raspberry pi, imu, and two speakers. The inner rig is displayed in the image below.

After the inner rig was designed, printed, and tested, the outer shell was designed. The shell needed to maintain a sleek outer appearance, while having inner structure to attach to the inner rig. The inside of the shell was designed to hold the inner rig in place, as well as secure the two halves of the shell together in a visually hidden way. Images of the CAD model of the Telemetron are displayed below.

Wine Rack

When I first moved home from MIT due to the pandemic, I was very upset. My manufacturing class went virtual, and I was not able to apply the knowledge learned in my classes to physically build something. Luckily, my father is a mechanical engineer, who owns a lot of manufacturing equipment. I decided to take on the task of building a wine rack for my parents! Here is some more details on this project!

Step 1: CAD Model

The first step was measuring the dimensions of the wine cellar. After knowing the dimensions I could start designing. After searching through pinterest for design inspiration, I landed on two designs. In Solidworks, I was able to combine the two designs into one wine rack, as shown in the CAD model below.

Step 2: Prototype

Next I built a mini prototype of an X (shown on the second shelf in the picture below) to ensure the saw cut designs would fit together correctly. Then I started building!

Step 3: Assemble

Next I built one side of the design. The bottom X was removed to store wine crates. Unfortunatly, I had to move before finishing the whole assembly, but will be completing it this winter! Updates coming soon!

Autonomous Robot

For my design and manufacturing class last Spring, the focus of the class was to design and build an autonomous robot to compete on a game board. Unfortunately, I was not able to build the robot I designed due to the pandemic, but in the first half of the semester I manufactured a prototype robot.

CAD Model

Below is the CAD model of the robot I designed for the competition. The sheet metal on the back of the robot would be used to push items around the game board, and the claw on the front would be used to rotate a wheel. I performed torque calculations to ensure the selected motor has enough torque to rotate the wheel. I also used Solidworks FEA analysis to ensure the sheet metal could withstand the applied force without plastic deformation.

Prototype

Before the pandemic, I manufactured a prototype robot pictured below. This robot could navigate the gameboard autonomously utilizing an ultrasonic sensor and line following.

Robot Arm 6 DOFs

My most recent project has been a robot arm with degrees of freedom. I assembled the robot arm, and have been testing the controller. Future development includes mounting the arm, and programming the arm to pick up objects using the claw.
