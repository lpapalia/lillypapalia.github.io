Wine Rack

When I first moved home from MIT due to the pandemic, I was very upset. My manufacturing class went virtual, and I was not able to apply the knowledge learned in my classes to physically build something. Luckily, my father is a mechanical engineer and has many tools and manufacturing equiptment. I decided to take on the task of building a wine rack for my parents! Here is some more details on this project!

Step 1: CAD Model

The first step was measuring the dimensions of the wine cellar. After knowing the dimensions I could start designing. After searching through pinterst for design inspirations I landed on two designs. In Solidworks, I was able to combine the two designs into one wine rack, as shown in the CAD model below.

Step 2: Prototype

Next I built a mini prototype of an X (shown on the second shelf in the picture below) to ensure the saw cut designs would fit together correctly. Then I started building!

Step 3: Assemble

Next I build one side of the design. The bottom X was removed to store wine crates. Unfortunatly, I had to move before finishing the whole assembly, but will be completeing it this winter! Updates coming soon!

Autonomous Robot

For my design and manufacturing class last Spring, the focus of the class is to design and build an autonomous robot to compete on a game board. Unfortunatly, I was not able to build the robot I designed due to the pandemic, but in the first half of the semester I namufactured a prototype robot.

CAD Model

Below is the CAD model of the robot I desgined for the competition. The sheet metal on the back of the robot would be used to psuh items around the gaem board, and the claw on the front woul dbe used to rotate a wheel. I performed torque calculations to ensure the selected motor has enough torque to rotate the wheel. I also used Solidworks FEA analysis to ensure the sheet metal could withstand the applied force without deformations.

Prototype

Before the pandemic, I manufactured a prototype robot pictured below. This robot could navigate the gameboard autonomously utilizing an ultrasonic sensor and line following.

Robot Arm 6 DOFs

My most recent project has been a robot arm with degrees of freedom. I assembled the robot arm, and have been testing the controller. Future development includes mounting the arm, and programming the arm to pick up objects using the claw.
